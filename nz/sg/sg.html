What do the Graphs Show?

  • The bar-graph’s YELLOW bars show the amount of electrical energy (not power) generated by the school’s Solar PV array. Generally this is used directly by the school by appliances such as lights, heaters, computers, fridges etc.
  • The pie-graph represents where the electrical energy used by the school comes from in what- the YELLOW pie segment shows the percentage of solar energy generated by the school in comparison to how much is imported from the Grid (ORANGE pie segment).

Navigation for graphs

  • To zoom out: two-finger double-tap anywhere on bar-graph.
  • To select a range: tap once on any bar you wish to start on, tap again on the bar you wish to end on.
  • Tap on Today button to see today's energy in high detail.
  • Tap on Lifetime button to see total energy measured by the system.
  • To view solar and grid energy together, tap on the Show more button.

Show more

  • The ORANGE bars shows the amount of energy passing into, or out of, the main electrical switch-board at the school. If it is entering the school from the Grid (being imported) it is below the line (x-axis).
  • Shows the CO2 avoided by generating electricity with solar panels, as well as the CO2 emitted to produce that amount of energy from the grid (due to fossil fuel power stations).
  • Electrical energy is typically measured in kilowatt-hours (kWh) and this is what the consumer pays for (along with fixed line charges).
  • If the ORANGE bar is above the line, this shows the amount of solar generated electricity leaving the school to the Grid (being exported). Unless the school has a large solar array or is small and very energy efficient it will not export much, or any, of its electricity. Look for sunny days during the weekend to see if you can spot any solar export to the Grid!
